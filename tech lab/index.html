01 Project Overview

🎯

Objective

Design a compact, real-time Air Quality Index monitoring system capable of detecting harmful gases and particulate matter concentrations in indoor and outdoor environments.

📡

Connectivity

Built-in Wi-Fi and Bluetooth via ESP32 enables wireless data transmission to cloud dashboards or local display units for remote monitoring.

⚡

Power

Low-power design operating at 3.3V–5V, suitable for battery-powered deployments with sleep mode capabilities for extended field operation.

🔬

Sensing

Dual-sensor architecture combining gas detection (NH₃, CO₂, Benzene, NOₓ) and particulate matter (PM2.5) measurement for comprehensive air quality analysis.

03 Component Details

MCU

ESP32 DevKit V1

ProcessorXtensa Dual-Core 32-bit LX6, 240 MHz
Memory520 KB SRAM, 4 MB Flash
WirelessWi-Fi 802.11 b/g/n + Bluetooth 4.2 BLE
GPIO34 programmable pins (ADC, DAC, PWM, I2C, SPI)
ADC12-bit SAR ADC, up to 18 channels
Operating Voltage3.3V (5V USB input)
Role: Central processing unit — reads analog sensor data, processes AQI calculations, and transmits results via Wi-Fi/BLE.
GAS SENSOR

MQ-135 Gas Sensor

DetectionNH₃, NOₓ, CO₂, Benzene, Alcohol, Smoke
Range10–1000 ppm (varies by gas)
OutputAnalog voltage (0–3.3V after divider)
Heater Voltage5V
Preheat Time24–48 hrs (initial), ~5 min (subsequent)
Operating Temp-10°C to 45°C
Role: Detects harmful gas concentrations — analog output is proportional to gas concentration, read via ESP32 ADC.
DUST SENSOR

GP2Y1010AU0F — PM2.5 Particle Sensor

DetectionFine particles (PM2.5), dust, smoke
Sensitivity0.5 V per 100 µg/m³
OutputAnalog voltage (proportional to dust density)
LED Pulse0.32ms pulse, 10ms cycle
Supply Voltage5V (with 150Ω + 220µF on LED)
Current Draw~20 mA (including LED)
Role: Measures particulate matter density — uses an infrared LED and phototransistor to detect light scattered by dust particles.
