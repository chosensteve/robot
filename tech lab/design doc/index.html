01 Project Overview

🎯

Objective

Design a compact, real-time Air Quality Index monitoring system capable of detecting harmful gases and particulate matter concentrations in indoor and outdoor environments.

📡

Connectivity

Built-in Wi-Fi and Bluetooth via ESP32 enables wireless data transmission to cloud dashboards or local display units for remote monitoring.

⚡

Power

Low-power design operating at 3.3V–5V, suitable for battery-powered deployments with sleep mode capabilities for extended field operation.

🔬

Sensing

Dual-sensor architecture combining gas detection (NH₃, CO₂, Benzene, NOₓ) and particulate matter (PM2.5) measurement for comprehensive air quality analysis.

03 Component Details

MCU

ESP32 DevKit V1

The ESP32 is the brain of the system. It reads data from both sensors, calculates the Air Quality Index, and sends the results wirelessly to a phone or dashboard via Wi-Fi.
GAS SENSOR

MQ-135 Gas Sensor

The MQ-135 detects harmful gases in the air such as CO₂, ammonia, benzene, and CO. It outputs an analog voltage that increases with gas concentration, which the ESP32 reads to determine air quality.
DUST SENSOR

PM2.5 Particle Sensor

The PM2.5 sensor detects fine dust and smoke particles in the air using an infrared LED and a light detector. The ESP32 reads the output to measure how many particles are present per cubic metre.
